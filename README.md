
## Abstract
<font size="10">
<div align="justify">
<ul>
  <li>Skin diseases pose significant challenges in terms of quick, accurate diagnosis, especially in regions with limited healthcare resources. Early detection is crucial for improving outcomes, particularly for conditions like eczema, dermatitis, melanoma, and psoriasis. Photonics and laser-based medical technologies offer faster and more precise diagnosis, but costs remain high. Automatic detection of dermoscopy image lesions faces challenges due to complex backgrounds and lesion features. Prior solutions have focused on large, complex models, but intra-class differences and inter-class similarity of lesion features require further investigation. Skin cancer, particularly melanoma, presents a significant health risk and early detection is key to improving survival rates. Machine learning offers cost-effective solutions for early skin cancer detection, assisting dermatologists in diagnosis. Skin lesions, including melanoma, contribute to high mortality rates globally, exacerbated by factors like population growth and environmental pollution. Early diagnosis is critical for reducing mortality, and advances in deep learning provide promising tools for early skin disease detection. In this proposed research paper, we aim to develop and introduce a method that enhances the accuracy and effectiveness of skin lesion detection and classification systems. By implementing advanced techniques such as Transfer Learning, fine-tuning, and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), we seek to improve model performance across diverse datasets. Additionally, image pre-processing techniques will be applied to enhance image quality, while data augmentation methods will generate additional training data and address issues such as imbalanced class distribution and dark skin detection. Leveraging Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI), we aim to provide interpretability and reliability to the model's classifications. Our goal is to develop a robust and adaptable system capable of accurately identifying a wide range of skin lesions, mitigating biases and noise in the data, and ensuring reliable performance across various scenarios.</li>
</ul>
</div></font>
